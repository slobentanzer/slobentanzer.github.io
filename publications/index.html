Publications

A list of my publications with short summaries

preprint: Lobentanzer S, Saez-Rodriguez J (2023) A platform for the biomedical application of Large Language Models arXiv, doi: https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.06488

BioChatter is an extension of the BioCypher ecosystem that enables the modular integration of Large Language Models into biomedical workflows. Its prototype frontend, ChatGSE demonstrates some capabilities of the framework in facilitating human-machine collaboration.

paper: Lobentanzer S, , Saez-Rodriguez J (2023) Democratizing knowledge representation with BioCypher Nature Biotechnology, 41, 1056–1059, doi: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-023-01848-y

Publication introducing the BioCypher framework, a modular and extensible knowledge representation framework written in Python. The framework is designed to be accessible to non-experts and to facilitate the integration of knowledge from different sources.

review: Garrido-Rodriguez M, Zirngibl K, Ivanova O, Lobentanzer S, Saez-Rodriguez J (2022) Integrating knowledge and omics to decipher mechanisms via large-scale models of signaling networks Molecular Systems Biology, 18:e11036, doi: https://doi.org/10.15252/msb.202211036

An overview of computational methods to evaluate signal transduction at the protein interaction layer. Briefly, the methods are heterogeneous in design as well as in purpose, and a medium-term objective should be the harmonisation and benchmarking of these methods to establish a baseline for the performance of signal propagation network modelling in molecular biology.

paper: Erbacher C, Vaknine S, Moshitzky G, Lobentanzer S, Eisenberg L, Evdokimov D, Sommer C, Greenberg DS, Soreq H, Üçeyler N (2022) Distinct CholinomiR Blood Cell Signature as a Potential Modulator of the Cholinergic System in Women with Fibromyalgia Syndrome Cells, 11(8), 1276; doi: https://doi.org/10.3390/cells11081276

Investigation into the small RNA species involved in cholinergic regulation of fibromyalgia.

monograph: Lobentanzer S (2020) Small RNA Dynamics in Cholinergic Systems. Dissertation, defended 2nd of October 2020 (summa cum laude), available at http://publikationen.ub.uni-frankfurt.de/frontdoor/index/index/docId/56125

The magnum opus from my PhD. In large parts related to the 2019 Cell Reports paper and the 2020 PNAS paper.

paper: Lobentanzer S, Klein J, Soreq H (2020) Establishing human male and female models of cholinergic neurons via neurokine-mediated differentiation of LA-N-2 and LA-N-5 neuroblastoma cells. STAR Protocols, 1 (3), doi: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.xpro.2020.100193

Elaboration on the cell culture developed for the 2019 Cell Reports paper.

paper: Winek K†, Lobentanzer S†, Nadorp B, Dubnov S, Dames C, Jagdmann S, Moshitzky G, Hotter B, Meisel C, Greenberg DS, Shifman S, Klein J, Shenhar-Tsarfaty S, Meisel A, Soreq H (2020) Transfer RNA fragments replace microRNA regulators of the cholinergic post-stroke immune blockade. Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci., 117 (51) 32606-32616, doi: https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2013542117 (†: equal contribution)

The second major publication resulting from my PhD. Small RNA species observed after a sizeable infarction in the brain of human patients show distinct pattern shifts between microRNAs and tRNA-derived fragments. While the functional implications are as yet unclear, tRNA fragments are an interesting emerging regulatory mechanism with potential of fast (immediate early-type) response times. We elucidated the background of several candidate molecules found and developed ways of validating their relevance in human- and mouse-derived experiments.

paper: Yuliani T, Lobentanzer S, Klein J (2020) Central cholinergic function and metabolic changes in streptozotocin-induced rat brain injury. Journal of Neurochemistry, 00: 1–13, doi: https://doi.org/10.1111/jnc.15155

Streptozoticin is a toxic agent used primarily in diabetes research due to its specific pancreatic beta-cell toxicity. However, there have been descriptions of emergent models using the agent to produce a phenotype in the central nervous system, to induce a dementia phenotype in rats. Briefly, we show that the cholinergic and metabolic phenotypes induced by intraventricular injection of Streptozotocin do not closely reproduce the phenotype commonly associated with dementia.

preprint: Lobentanzer S (2020) Scavenging of Interleukin 6 Receptor by Bioidentical Recombinant gp130 as Intervention in Covid-19 Exacerbation. Not peer-reviewed, doi: https://dx.doi.org/10.31219/osf.io/3gwmp

Scavenging of secreted IL-6 receptor protein has been proposed as a remedy for inflammatory processes fuelled by endocrine processes. Here I extend this idea to the systemic inflammation observed in Covid-19.

paper: Lobentanzer S, Hanin G, Klein J, Soreq H (2019) Integrative Transcriptomics Reveals Sexually Dimorphic Control of the Cholinergic/Neurokine Interface in Schizophrenia and Bipolar Disorder. Cell Reports 29(3):764-777.e5, doi: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.celrep.2019.09.017

The first major publication resulting from my PhD. Informed by a meta-analysis of human brain transcriptome samples and single-cell expression patterns, we develop a human neuronal cell culture system to study small RNA dynamics under cholinergic differentiation caused by neurokines. We identify families of microRNAs related to the differentiation process, which we cross-reference with disease processes in psychiatric diseases. In vitro interventional experiments confirm predictions of the pivotal microRNA miR-125b-5p in transcriptional cholinergic regulation.

preprint: Lobentanzer S (2020) Why most pre-published research findings are false. Not peer-reviewed, doi: https://doi.org/10.31219/osf.io/29jnp

A slightly humouristic take on the famous paper by John Ioannidis and the current surge in preprint publications, particularly in relation to the propensity of some news outlets to cherry-pick unreviewed articles for sensational reproduction.

book chapter: Lobentanzer S & Klein J (2019) Zentrales und Peripheres Nervensystem. In Wichmann & Fromme (Verleger), Handbuch für Umweltmedizin, Kapitel 11, ecomed Medizin, erg. lfg.

A book chapter on environmental toxicants and their specific effects on the human nervous system.

paper: Stein C, Koch K, Hopfeld J, Lobentanzer S, Lau H, Klein J (2019) Impaired hippocampal and thalamic acetylcholine release in P301L tau-transgenic mice. Brain Research Bulletin 152:134-142, doi: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.brainresbull.2019.07.014

We studied the release of acetylcholine in a mouse model of Alzheimer’s disease. The mice showed reduced acetylcholine levels in hippocampus and thalamus, which was further reduced after injection of Amyloid oligomers.
